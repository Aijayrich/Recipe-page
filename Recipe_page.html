African Breadfruit (Ukwa)

African breadfruit or ukwa in Igbo, is an edible traditional fruit. It belongs to the Moraceae family and is related to other exotic fruits like breadnut, jackfruit and figs. In Nigeria, it is eaten as a main dish. It is of a high nutritional value and is highly recommended for diabetics as it helps to lower blood sugar

Ingredients and measurements

Ingredients Measurement
Ukwa seeds 2 cups
Fresh peppers 5 large pieces
palm oil 1 cooking spoon
Ground Crayfish 3 table spoons
smoked catfish 2 pieces
seasoning cube 2 cubes
Scent leaves A handful
salt To taste

Preparations:

  1. Firstly, pick out stones from the ukwa sedds then wash and place the seeds in a pot

  2. Cook till they are well softened. (You may add potash to shorten the cooking time)

  3. Debone the catfish and wash in salted warm water

  4. When the ukwa seeds are softened, add the washed fish, crayfish, peppers, seasoning cubes, salt an palm oil.

  5. Cover the pot and allow to cook for about 10 minutes

  6. Taste and add more seasoning if required

  7. Add the washed and diced scent leaves, stir and turn off heat.

  8. Our delicious ukwa is ready to be served
